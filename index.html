What the model knows about your body

Body water volume
—
Alcohol spreads through your body water. More water = lower BAC for the same dose.
✓ Exact
Lean body mass
—
Your metabolically active tissue. Heavier lean mass = more liver capacity.
✓ Exact
Stomach emptying speed
—
How fast alcohol leaves your stomach. Food slows this — fat most of all.
✓ Reliable (R²=0.90)
Liver max speed (Vmax)
—
Peak speed your liver destroys alcohol. Varies genetically. This is your body-size estimate.
~ Body size estimate
Liver saturation point (Km)
—
BAC at which your liver works at half speed. Above this, alcohol piles up faster than it clears.
~ Body size estimate
Blood → brain speed
—
How fast alcohol crosses into your brain. Near-constant across people — similar for almost everyone.
~ Near-constant
Important. This is a research model, not a medical tool. Numbers are estimates based on physiological equations validated against published human studies (Jones & Jonsson, 1994 — MAE 0.009% BAC). Individual variation exists — genetics, medications, tolerance, and hydration all affect real BAC. Never use this to decide whether to drive.
